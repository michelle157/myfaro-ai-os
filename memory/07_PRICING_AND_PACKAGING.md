# 07 - Pricing & Packaging

## Current pricing

### Essential
- EUR 10,000 per year
- includes services as currently described
- dossier scope depends on agreement

### Professional
- EUR 13,000 per year
- up to 200 dossiers

### Above 200 dossiers
- Essential: + EUR 15 per dossier per year
- Professional: + EUR 25 per dossier per year

### Onboarding fee
- EUR 3,750 one-time onboarding fee

### Passive dossiers
- EUR 3 per passive dossier per year
- passive dossiers do not count in the over-200 active dossier calculation

## Discount policy
Known BZB-related discount logic:
- BZB member benefit: 15% license discount
- BZB webinar context: 50% onboarding discount and 15% license discount

Do not invent discounts. Any discount must be tied to an approved campaign, partner arrangement or explicit commercial decision.

## Contract duration
Default commercial model is annual. Six-month contract requests have previously not been accepted as standard. Commercial reasoning: myFaro is an operating layer requiring onboarding, configuration and adoption, so a short trial does not fit the implementation nature of the product.

## POC logic
A POC can be considered selectively, but it should not undermine the annual SaaS model. A valid POC has reduced scope, clear success criteria, a defined timeframe, an internal champion, a real data set and a commercial follow-through if successful.

Good POC use cases:
- portfolio overview for selected clients
- pension workflow for sample cases
- compliance onboarding workflow
- reporting output validation

Avoid POCs that are free explorations without decision path, require custom development before commitment, are too broad, lack an internal owner or postpone a decision indefinitely.

## Packaging logic

### Essential positioning
Use for offices that need a structured starting point and want to professionalize core workflows without immediately requiring all advanced capabilities. Fit: smaller broker offices, first milestone around portfolio overview, compliance or pension workflow entry.

### Professional positioning
Use for offices with broader life/investment activity, more dossiers, more reporting needs, deeper workflow requirements or stronger scalability ambitions. Fit: mature brokers, wealth planners, larger offices, groups and more complex compliance/reporting use cases.

## Pricing value argument
Pricing should be defended through operational leverage rather than feature count.

Value drivers:
- reduced manual reporting work
- improved compliance consistency
- less dependence on individual advisor discipline
- faster pension calculations
- better portfolio visibility
- structured client follow-up
- ability to grow life/investment revenue
- better client experience
- scalability across advisors

## ROI framing

### Time savings
Fewer manual portfolio overviews, fewer insurer portal checks, less Excel work, faster report generation and less manual document assembly.

### Revenue growth
Better identification of pension opportunities, better follow-up, deeper advice conversations and improved retention through professional reporting.

### Risk reduction
More consistent compliance process, audit trail, better evidence preservation and fewer missing documents/steps.

### Scalability
Easier onboarding of advisors, standardized process, more delegation and less dependence on senior advisors for routine work.

## Pricing objections

### “This is expensive.”
The relevant comparison is not a lightweight app. myFaro becomes part of the operating model for life, investments, pensions, compliance and reporting. The question is how much time, risk and commercial opportunity is currently lost in manual workflows and fragmented systems.

### “Can we pay monthly?”
The default model is annual because myFaro requires onboarding and becomes part of the advisory workflow. We can scope the right first milestone, but the commercial basis remains annual.

### “Can we start with six months?”
A short six-month setup does not reflect implementation and adoption effort. If there is uncertainty, scope a focused POC with success criteria rather than weaken the operating-layer commitment.

### “Can passive dossiers be excluded?”
Passive dossiers are treated differently: EUR 3 per passive dossier per year and they do not count in the over-200 active dossier calculation.

### “We are BZB members.”
Apply the BZB member benefit according to approved discount logic. Do not add extra concessions unless explicitly approved.

## Proposal structure
A proposal should include:
1. context and use case
2. selected package
3. annual license
4. onboarding fee
5. dossier scope
6. passive dossier treatment
7. approved discount if applicable
8. implementation scope
9. assumptions
10. exclusions
11. next step

## Commercial guardrails
- no bespoke pricing without approval
- no unlimited dossiers unless contracted
- no casual onboarding waiver
- no custom development inside standard license unless agreed
- no legal interpretation of terms without review
- no unconfirmed integration promises

## Terms and data export communication
When prospects challenge terms, clarify practical concerns without rewriting everything. For data export, be constructive:
> myFaro does not aim to create unnecessary lock-in. At the end of the collaboration, the relevant data export can be organized in a practical format, subject to the technical and contractual context.
