# 05 - Compliance & Regulation

## Compliance positioning
myFaro should be positioned as a compliance-sensitive workflow layer, not as a legal guarantee. Good language: supports a structured compliance process, embeds compliance steps in the advisory workflow, helps create audit-ready evidence, helps reproduce the advisory process, reduces manual document handling and supports consistent KYC, AML, risk profiling and advice documentation.

Avoid absolute claims: guarantees compliance, FSMA-proof, eliminates regulatory risk, replaces legal review, automatic compliance in all cases.

## Relevant regulatory domains

### IDD / AssurMiFID
Insurance distribution and investment insurance advice require structured client profiling, suitability reasoning, product understanding and evidence. myFaro supports this through structured client data, risk profile, knowledge and experience, product selection rationale, document triggers and audit trail.

### AML / KYC
Advisors must identify clients, understand risk factors, document beneficial ownership where relevant and preserve evidence. myFaro can support onboarding workflows, KYC collection, AML risk assessment, company data through Creditsafe where applicable, document storage and audit trail.

### PRIIPs and product documentation
Investment products and insurance-based investment products may require specific disclosures or product documents. myFaro can support document triggers and structured file evidence.

### Pension / IPT / 80%-rule
Corporate pension advice requires structured calculation and documentation. The 80%-rule is a recurring operational pain. myFaro centralizes company data, remuneration inputs, pension contracts, 80%-rule calculations and advice documentation.

### GDPR and data privacy
myFaro processes personal and financial data. It should be described carefully as a processor or technology provider depending on the contractual context. Known context: myFaro often operates as data processor for brokers; DPA/DPIA topics have been discussed; a Torion audit was referenced; myFaro is not positioned as directly under DORA in earlier reasoning; SharePoint-based vault logic supports document portability and governance.

## Compliance as workflow, not paperwork
Traditional compliance often becomes separate forms, manual checklists, scattered evidence, inconsistent user behavior and difficult file reconstruction. myFaro’s stronger story is that required steps appear in the relevant workflow, irrelevant questions are avoided, documents are triggered by context, actions create audit trail and evidence is stored structurally.

This is the difference between “having documents” and “having a reproducible process”.

## Audit trail
Audit trail is commercially important. It can evidence user actions, timestamps, document generation, client acceptance/signature events, workflow completion, data changes and access events where logging exists.

Use: “myFaro helps preserve structured evidence around what was done, when and by whom.”

Avoid: “myFaro makes the broker fully compliant.”

## FIMIS / reproducibility language
A useful compliance phrase is reproducibility. The advisory firm should be able to reconstruct how advice was prepared and documented. myFaro supports reproducibility through common workflows, structured data capture, traceable documents, audit trail and consistent reports.

## AML/KYC external wording
Recommended:
> myFaro helps advisors structure the AML/KYC process by collecting relevant client and company data, triggering required checks and preserving evidence in the client file.

Avoid:
> myFaro fully automates AML compliance.

## AssurMiFID external wording
Recommended:
> myFaro supports a more consistent advisory process by linking client profile, risk profile, product information, advice documentation and audit trail in one workflow.

Avoid:
> myFaro guarantees suitability.

## 80%-rule external wording
Recommended:
> myFaro centralizes the 80%-rule calculation inside the pension advisory workflow, so advisors do not need to rely on fragmented insurer portals or Excel files for every case.

Be careful not to imply that every legal nuance is automatically solved without human review.

## Cross-border compliance
When discussing OneLife, Utmost or cross-border life insurance, use cautious language:
> The workflow can support structured data collection and documentation, but the exact cross-border legal and tax treatment should be validated with the relevant experts.

## Data export and contract concerns
When customers ask about data export at termination, be constructive: confirm that export can be organized, clarify format and practical process, avoid unnecessary lock-in, but do not rewrite standard terms casually.

Suggested wording:
> We understand the concern around data portability. myFaro does not aim to create unnecessary lock-in. At the end of the collaboration, the relevant data export can be organized in a practical format to be agreed, subject to the technical and contractual context.

## DORA / DPA / DPIA positioning
Use caution. The exact DORA qualification depends on role and contractual context. In current positioning, myFaro is primarily a technology provider/data processor for advisory firms. DPA/DPIA responses should define data categories, processing roles, sub-processors if relevant, security controls at a high level and SharePoint/Microsoft architecture where appropriate.

## Compliance agent rules
1. Avoid legal absolutes.
2. Use supports, helps, structures, documents, embeds.
3. Distinguish workflow support from legal responsibility.
4. Do not invent regulatory requirements.
5. Recommend expert validation for legal interpretation.
6. Keep messaging commercially useful but cautious.
