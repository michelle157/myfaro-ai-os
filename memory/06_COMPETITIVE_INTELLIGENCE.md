# 06 - Competitive Intelligence

## Competitive thesis
myFaro should not compete as a narrow feature tool. It should be positioned as the horizontal operating layer for life, investment, pension, compliance and reporting workflows.

Core competitive frame:
> Many tools solve one part of the advisory process. myFaro connects the process across client data, contracts, compliance, advice, pension calculations, reporting and multi-supplier collaboration.

## Competitor categories

### CRM and BOAR systems
Examples: Brio, Brokercloud, HubSpot and other broker CRM systems.

Their strengths: relationship management, pipeline, non-life administration, broad office administration, existing user adoption.

Their weaknesses in myFaro’s context: limited depth for life/investment/pension workflows, limited consolidated portfolio reporting, compliance evidence often manual, weak 80%-rule workflow, not designed as an advice workflow engine.

Positioning: do not attack the CRM. Say the CRM remains the relationship/admin layer, while myFaro adds the operating layer for life, investments, pensions, compliance and reporting.

### Pension or insurance ERP tools
Examples: e-Gor, Igor, Egor, and in some contexts Dory.

Their strengths: pension or insurance workflow specialization.

myFaro distinction: broader than pension administration; combines reporting, portfolio overview, compliance, multi-supplier advisory process and client relationship layer.

Positioning: if the need is only narrow pension administration, a pension-specific tool may be considered. If the office wants to structure life, investments, compliance, reporting and portfolio insight in one operating layer, myFaro is the more strategic conversation.

### Financial planning and estate planning tools
Example: Abbove.

Their strengths: financial planning, estate planning, simulations, planning conversations.

myFaro distinction: stronger operating workflow around insurance, investments, pension, compliance evidence, contract/portfolio data and reporting.

Positioning: Abbove can be relevant when the main need is planning. myFaro is broader when the office needs an operating workflow for life, investments, pensions, compliance and reporting.

### Portfolio and investment reporting tools
Examples: Fundshop, Finary and other reporting/aggregation tools.

Their strengths: portfolio view, analytics, consumer-style dashboard or investment reporting.

myFaro distinction: reporting is embedded in a professional advisory workflow including insurance wrappers, pension context, compliance evidence, documents, audit trail and advisor-owned client file.

Positioning: portfolio analytics are only one layer. myFaro connects investment insight to client file, compliance workflow, contract context and reporting process.

### Supplier portals
Examples: insurer and bank portals.

Their strengths: official product data, product-specific execution, documents and calculations.

Their weaknesses: fragmented by supplier, not advisor-owned, no consolidated client view, weak cross-supplier reporting, inefficient for multi-supplier advisors and strategically risky because client interaction moves toward supplier environment.

Positioning: supplier portals remain useful, but they should not become the advisor’s operating model. myFaro lets advisors work across suppliers from their own environment.

## Battlecards

### “We already have Brio.”
Response: Brio can remain the core BOAR/admin system. myFaro handles deeper life, pension, investment, compliance and reporting workflows.

Proof points: portfolio overview across suppliers, 80%-rule workflow, compliance evidence, reporting and client environment.

### “We are looking at Abbove.”
Response: Abbove is planning-oriented. myFaro is an operating layer for the broader advisory process around insurance, investments, pensions, compliance and reporting.

Proof points: contract and portfolio data, workflow and audit trail, supplier-agnostic reporting.

### “We use insurer portals.”
Response: supplier portals solve supplier-specific tasks. myFaro solves the advisor’s cross-supplier operating problem.

Proof points: consolidated view, advisor-owned client environment, less manual coordination.

### “We want a reporting tool.”
Response: reporting is stronger when connected to client data, contracts, compliance and advice. myFaro is not just a dashboard.

Proof points: consolidated reporting, advisory workflow, document output, audit trail.

### “Dory/Brokercloud/Igor also cover this.”
Response: clarify which layer they cover. If they are CRM, BOAR or pension-specific, position myFaro as the dedicated operating layer for life, investments, pensions, compliance and reporting across suppliers.

## Pricing comparison logic
Do not compete as a cheap app. Defend pricing through operating value:
- AUM visibility
- time saved in reporting and compliance
- ability to grow life/investment business
- reduced manual work
- improved client professionalism
- scalability across advisors
- better evidence and reproducibility

## Market timing
The market is moving from single-function tools to integrated operating workflows because compliance is more demanding, clients expect consolidated reporting, broker groups are consolidating, staff is scarce and multi-supplier advice creates data fragmentation.

myFaro should own the horizontal operating-layer narrative before competitors converge on it.

## Safe comparative language
Use: different focus, more narrow use case, planning-oriented, CRM-oriented, supplier-specific, analytics-focused, pension-specific, myFaro is broader when the challenge is operating model and workflow.

Avoid: inferior, obsolete, cannot do X unless verified, legal accusations, unverified feature claims.

## Intelligence gaps to monitor
Track competitor pricing, new features, CRM/insurer integrations, AI capabilities, compliance workflow updates, market consolidation and partnerships with associations or insurers. Do not invent current facts. When asked for recent competitor information, use up-to-date research.
