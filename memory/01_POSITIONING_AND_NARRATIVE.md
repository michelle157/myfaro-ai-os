# 01 - Positioning & Narrative

## Strategic category
myFaro should be positioned as the **operating layer** or **ERP layer** for independent financial advisors. This is the category-design anchor. The market already has CRMs, BOAR tools, insurer portals, bank portals, document generators, financial planning tools, compliance checklists and reporting dashboards. The strategic problem is not a lack of software. The problem is that advisory firms lack one structured workflow connecting client data, contracts, portfolio insight, advice, compliance, reporting and follow-up across multiple suppliers.

myFaro fills that horizontal gap.

## Core positioning
myFaro is the CRM-agnostic operating layer for independent financial advisors who need one structured workflow for client data, portfolio insight, compliance, advice and reporting across multiple banks, insurers and investment structures.

This positioning must remain consistent in sales, website copy, LinkedIn, partner communication and demo narratives.

## What myFaro is
myFaro is:
- a CRM-agnostic advisory infrastructure layer
- a structured workflow environment for life insurance, investment insurance, pensions and wealth advisory
- a multi-supplier layer for advisors working with insurers, banks and investment structures
- a compliance-sensitive workflow engine with audit trail
- a reporting and portfolio insight layer
- a scalability layer for advisory firms that want to grow without adding proportional manual work

## What myFaro is not
Do not reduce myFaro to a portal, document generator, pension calculator, reporting dashboard, compliance checklist, CRM replacement, supplier portal or AI wrapper. The word “platform” can be used if needed, but the preferred words are operating layer, ERP layer, enablement layer and advisory infrastructure.

## Market problem
Independent advisors face pressure from five directions:

1. regulatory complexity around AML/KYC, IDD, AssurMiFID, suitability, auditability and file evidence
2. fragmented workflows across banks, insurers, CRMs, PDFs, Excel files and supplier portals
3. client expectations around consolidated reporting and digital access
4. margin and staff pressure, making manual processes expensive
5. consolidation in the broker and wealth advisory market, creating a need for scalable operating models

The strategic thesis is: the advisory firms that will scale are the firms that structure their workflows and data first.

## Narrative by ICP

### Independent insurance brokers
For brokers with meaningful life and investment business, myFaro is the life and investment ERP layer next to Brio, Brokercloud or another CRM/BOAR system. Their pain is often that life, pensions and investment advice still happen through insurer portals, PDFs, Excel, ad hoc documents and individual advisor habits.

Core message: keep your client relationship in your own advisory environment and use myFaro to structure life, pension, investment, compliance and reporting workflows across suppliers.

### Bank agents and bank brokers
For bank agents, myFaro adds a broader financial planning, life insurance, pension and reporting layer outside the limits of the core banking environment.

Core message: myFaro gives bank agents a structured advisory layer for planning, life insurance, pension and investment reporting without locking the client view into one supplier.

### Wealth planners and family offices
For wealth planners and family offices, the need is a consolidated view across banking assets, investment insurance, private equity, companies, maatschappen, real estate and estate structures.

Core message: myFaro helps move from scattered portfolio fragments and documents to a consolidated advisory view that supports reporting, planning and follow-up.

### Buy-and-build groups
Consolidators inherit different systems, habits, CRMs, supplier relations and compliance practices. They need a shared operating model without forcing every entity into the same stack immediately.

Core message: myFaro gives the group a shared operating layer for life and investment advisory workflows while allowing local entities to keep existing tools where useful.

### Insurers and banks as partners
Suppliers want more efficient, compliant and data-driven distribution, but another supplier portal rarely solves the advisor’s operating problem.

Core message: myFaro helps suppliers enable advisors through structured workflows, data exchange and compliant distribution processes without taking the client relationship away from the advisor.

## Differentiators
1. **Horizontal rather than point solution**: myFaro connects client data, contracts, compliance, pension, reporting and advice instead of solving only one task.
2. **CRM-agnostic**: myFaro can sit next to HubSpot, Brio, Brokercloud or other systems.
3. **Multi-supplier**: myFaro is strongest where advisors work across multiple banks, insurers and investment structures.
4. **Workflow-driven**: documents and questions are triggered where relevant, not presented as endless lists.
5. **Compliance embedded in workflow**: compliance is part of the advisory path, supported by audit trail and structured evidence.
6. **Reporting linked to advice**: reporting is not a separate dashboard; it becomes part of the client relationship and advice process.

## Key narrative: workflow versus documents
A long list of documents is not a compliance or advisory process. Document thinking forces the advisor to know what applies, navigate templates and assemble evidence manually. Workflow thinking guides the advisor, triggers documents only when relevant, hides irrelevant questions and creates auditability while the work is being done.

This is a major USP. myFaro should be presented as the layer that makes complexity manageable by never making the workflow harder than necessary.

## Key narrative: next to the CRM
A CRM knows who the client is and where the relationship stands. myFaro structures what happens when the advisor needs to manage life insurance, investments, pension optimization, compliance evidence, underwriting, reporting or financial planning. The stronger position is not “replace the CRM”. The stronger position is: your CRM remains important, but myFaro adds the operating layer for the advisory work most CRMs were not designed to manage deeply.

## Key narrative: advisor-owned client environment
Supplier portals are useful for supplier-specific execution, but they fragment the client view and can shift control toward the insurer or bank. myFaro keeps the advisor in control: the client logs in through the advisor’s environment, reporting is advisor-branded and the workflow is not dictated by one supplier.

## Website language examples
**Hero**: The operating layer for independent financial advisors.

**Subline**: Bring client data, portfolio insight, compliance, advice workflows and reporting together across banks, insurers and investment structures.

**Broker page**: Life and investment advice has become too important to manage through scattered portals and manual files. myFaro gives brokers one workflow for portfolio overview, compliance, pension calculations, client reporting and advice follow-up.

**Partner page**: Enable your distribution network without forcing advisors into another isolated portal. myFaro helps banks and insurers collaborate with advisors through structured workflows, data exchange and audit-ready documentation.

## Risks to avoid
- Being seen as “another portal”: counter with advisor-owned workflow infrastructure across suppliers.
- Being seen as only compliance software: counter with portfolio insight, reporting, advice and scalability.
- Being seen as only pension software: counter with pension as one strong use case inside a broader life and investment operating layer.
- Being compared too narrowly to Abbove or Egor: counter with full advisory workflow across data, compliance, reporting and multi-supplier execution.
- Being perceived as too heavy: counter with phased implementation around one first milestone.
