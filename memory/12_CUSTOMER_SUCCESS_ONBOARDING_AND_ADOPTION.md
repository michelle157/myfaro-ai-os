# 12 - Customer Success, Onboarding & Adoption

## Customer success philosophy
myFaro is an operating layer. Adoption therefore depends on workflow change, not only software training. Customer success should focus on defining the first milestone, keeping scope realistic, making the advisor’s daily workflow easier, creating visible value early and expanding after adoption is proven.

## Onboarding principle
Do not onboard everything at once. Start with the use case that creates the clearest value.

Possible first milestones:
- complete overview of life and investment portfolio
- first client reporting workflow live
- compliance onboarding workflow live
- pension / 80%-rule workflow live
- first team trained on standard process
- first entity live in group rollout

## Onboarding stages

### 1. Kickoff
Goal: align scope, users, timeline and first milestone.

Inputs: package, users, data sources, current systems, chosen workflows, stakeholders and success criteria.

Output: implementation plan, responsibilities, data requirements and training plan.

### 2. Data and configuration
Goal: set up the environment and required data.

Activities: client/dossier import where applicable, user setup, roles, workflow configuration, document/vault setup and supplier/portfolio data preparation.

### 3. Training
Goal: train users around workflow, not just buttons.

Training should answer: when do I use myFaro, what workflow do I follow, which data must be complete, where do documents go, how is compliance evidence captured and what output do I generate?

### 4. First cases
Goal: use real client files to validate the workflow.

Activities: select representative cases, complete workflow, generate output, capture user feedback and fix process gaps.

### 5. Adoption review
Goal: confirm whether first milestone was reached and plan expansion.

Questions: are users using the workflow, which steps create friction, is data quality sufficient, does reporting meet expectations, are compliance steps understood and what workflow should be activated next?

## Adoption risks

### Too broad scope
Risk: customer wants everything at once, leading to slow adoption. Mitigation: define one first milestone and phase expansion.

### No internal champion
Risk: users do not change habits. Mitigation: identify champion before onboarding and involve them in first cases.

### Poor data quality
Risk: reports and workflows disappoint because source data is incomplete. Mitigation: set data expectations early and prioritize active dossiers.

### CRM confusion
Risk: users expect myFaro to replace all CRM work. Mitigation: explain division between CRM for relationship/pipeline and myFaro for life, investment, compliance and reporting workflows.

### Compliance fear
Risk: users think myFaro adds compliance burden. Mitigation: show how workflow reduces irrelevant questions and manual document assembly.

## Change management messaging
For users:
> myFaro is not meant to make your work heavier. The workflow is designed to show the relevant steps, trigger the right documents and keep evidence while you work.

For management:
> The value of myFaro is not only individual efficiency. It is a more consistent operating model across the office.

For compliance stakeholders:
> The value is structured evidence and reproducibility of the advisory process.

## CS playbooks by use case

### Portfolio overview onboarding
Success criteria: active contracts structured, advisor can view client portfolio, reporting output validated and missing data list identified.

Expansion: client reporting, follow-up campaigns and compliance workflows.

### Compliance onboarding
Success criteria: workflow configured, required documents triggered, users understand evidence logic and audit trail explained.

Expansion: broader onboarding, product-specific workflows and review cycles.

### Pension onboarding
Success criteria: pension data available, 80%-rule workflow tested, sample cases validated and advisors trained.

Expansion: pension optimization campaigns and company-owner segment follow-up.

### Reporting onboarding
Success criteria: report template validated, data sources mapped, sample reports produced and advisor commentary process defined.

Expansion: recurring reporting schedule, client portal activation and group dashboards.

## Customer health indicators
Healthy signals:
- active users log in regularly
- first milestone achieved
- reports generated
- workflows completed
- internal champion engaged
- support questions are workflow-specific
- expansion opportunities appear

Risk signals:
- no internal owner
- data import stalled
- users keep working outside myFaro
- management expectations differ from user reality
- repeated basic workflow confusion
- first milestone unclear

## Expansion logic
Expansion should follow demonstrated value. Common paths: portfolio overview to reporting, reporting to client portal, compliance workflow to broader onboarding, pension workflow to recurring pension campaigns, one team to full office and one entity to group rollout.

## Support and ticketing philosophy
Support should capture technical issues and workflow friction. Categories: bug, data issue, workflow question, training need, reporting issue, compliance/document issue, integration/source issue and change request. Recurring tickets should feed product and onboarding improvements.

## Customer communication examples
Kickoff:
> We starten met één duidelijke eerste mijlpaal, zodat de implementatie beheersbaar blijft en snel waarde toont. Nadien kunnen we uitbreiden naar bijkomende workflows zoals rapportering, compliance of pensioenadvies.

Adoption review:
> Het doel van deze review is te bekijken of de eerste workflow effectief werkt in jullie dagelijkse praktijk: welke stappen lopen goed, waar zit nog frictie en welke uitbreiding heeft nu het meeste commerciële of operationele waarde.

Change management:
> myFaro werkt het best wanneer het kantoor beslist welke workflow de standaard wordt. De software ondersteunt die manier van werken, maar adoptie vraagt ook een duidelijke interne afspraak.

## Agent instruction
When helping with onboarding or adoption: identify first milestone, avoid scope creep, separate technical setup from workflow adoption, preserve CRM-next-to-myFaro logic and focus on measurable operational value.
