# 04 - Sales Process & Playbooks

## Sales philosophy
myFaro should be sold consultatively. The buyer must feel that myFaro understands the operating reality of independent advisory firms. The conversation should not start with features, but with the workflow that is currently broken.

The sales process should identify where data is fragmented, where compliance creates manual effort, where supplier portals weaken the advisor relationship, where reporting is too manual and what first milestone would create measurable value.

## Sales process
1. lead qualification
2. discovery meeting
3. use-case framing
4. tailored demo
5. internal stakeholder alignment
6. proposal or POC
7. contracting
8. onboarding kickoff
9. adoption review
10. expansion

## Qualification framework

### Fit questions
- Do they advise on life insurance, investment insurance, pensions or wealth planning?
- Do they work with multiple insurers or banks?
- Which CRM, BOAR or document systems do they use?
- How do they produce reporting today?
- How do they document AML/KYC and advice evidence?
- How many active and passive dossiers do they manage?
- Is there an internal owner with authority?
- Is there budget for annual license and onboarding?

### High-fit signals
“We do not have a full overview.” “Everything is spread across portals.” “Compliance is too manual.” “Pension calculations take too much time.” “We want better client reporting.” “We are acquiring offices.” “Our CRM does not cover this depth.” “We need to stay in control of the client relationship.”

### Low-fit signals
“We only need a cheaper CRM.” “We want one document template.” “We do not do life or investment business.” “We need custom development before starting.” “We want a long free test.” “No one internally owns this.”

## Discovery structure

### Opening
Frame the meeting around operating model, not software:
> I suggest we first understand how your life, pension, investment and reporting workflows are organized today. Then we can decide whether myFaro should be shown from the angle of portfolio overview, compliance, pension advice or reporting.

### Current operating model
Ask about client segments, strategic activities, CRM/BOAR/document tools, supplier portals, where an advisor starts when preparing a file and which processes are still manual.

### Data and reporting
Ask whether they have a full overview of active life/investment contracts, whether they can report per client across suppliers, how they distinguish active/passive dossiers and how much Excel/PDF work exists.

### Compliance workflow
Ask how they handle AML/KYC, risk profile, knowledge and experience, suitability reasoning, evidence storage and file reproducibility.

### Pension workflow
Ask how they calculate the 80%-rule, whether they rely on insurer portals, how pension opportunities are detected and whether pension is a growth domain.

### Business impact
Ask what is too slow, what is hard to delegate, where rework happens, which milestone would make implementation worthwhile and who must support the change.

## Use-case framing after discovery
At the end of discovery, summarize the first use case clearly:

- **Portfolio overview**: reliable overview of life/investment contracts so reporting and follow-up become manageable.
- **Compliance workflow**: more consistent and auditable advice without more manual document work.
- **Pension workflow**: centralize pension advice and 80%-rule instead of scattered insurer portals.
- **Reporting**: professionalize client reporting across banks, insurers and investment structures.
- **Group standardization**: create one way of working across entities without forcing the same CRM everywhere.

## Demo principles
Never give a generic feature tour. Follow the buyer’s workflow.

Strong demo sequence:
1. start from the business problem
2. show client or portfolio overview
3. show guided workflow
4. show embedded compliance
5. show documents/reporting output
6. show audit trail/evidence logic
7. close with first milestone and implementation path

## Demo scripts by use case

### Portfolio overview
Narrative: Most offices do not lack client relationships; they lack a reliable operating view of what sits across insurers, banks and contracts. Without structured portfolio data, reporting and follow-up remain manual.

Show: client overview, contract overview, supplier view, values, active/passive dossiers, reporting output.

Close: first milestone is making life and investment portfolio visible and reportable.

### Compliance
Narrative: The point is not to add compliance work. The point is to make required steps part of the natural advisory workflow.

Show: onboarding, risk profile, AML/KYC, document triggers, evidence and audit trail.

Close: advisors follow the right steps without manually assembling the compliance file afterwards.

### Pension / 80%-rule
Narrative: Pension advice becomes fragmented when calculations sit in insurer portals or Excel. myFaro brings this into the advisory workflow.

Show: client/company link, pension contracts, 80%-rule, advice documentation and reporting.

Close: this creates a repeatable pension process that can be delegated and scaled.

### Reporting
Narrative: Reporting is not only a PDF. It is the moment where the advisor proves structure, overview and control.

Show: consolidated view, portfolio by supplier/product, route plan, PDF output and advisor commentary.

## Objection handling

### “We already have a CRM.”
Your CRM remains the relationship system. myFaro structures the life, pension, investment, compliance and reporting workflows that most CRMs do not manage deeply enough.

### “We already use Brio/Brokercloud.”
Brio or Brokercloud can remain the BOAR/admin layer. myFaro adds the advisory operating layer for life and investments, where work often still happens through portals, Excel and documents.

### “This seems heavy.”
Implementation should start with one milestone: portfolio overview, compliance workflow, pension calculation or reporting. The workflow reduces irrelevant questions and documents.

### “Can we test for six months?”
The default model is annual because myFaro is an operating layer. If needed, a focused POC can be scoped with success criteria, but not as an open-ended free trial.

### “Can you customize this?”
First check whether the need fits product direction and standard workflows. Custom work should only be considered when it strengthens the broader product and business case.

### “Your terms favor myFaro.”
The terms are standard SaaS terms, but practical points such as data export at termination, support process and responsibilities can be clarified. The principle is not to create unnecessary lock-in.

## Follow-up email structure
1. thank them
2. name the concrete pain/use case
3. translate myFaro into 2-3 relevant outcomes
4. confirm next step

Example:
> Beste [naam], dank voor het aangename gesprek over jullie werking rond leven, beleggingen en rapportering. Ik begreep vooral dat jullie meer structuur willen in contracten en klantdata, zodat rapportering en opvolging minder afhankelijk worden van manueel werk en losse portalen. In de demo tonen we daarom concreet hoe myFaro als operating layer portefeuilledata, compliance en rapportering in één workflow samenbrengt. Ik bevestig graag de volgende stap.

## POC rules
A valid POC has clear scope, first milestone, limited users, defined data set, success criteria, end date and commercial consequence if successful. Avoid broad unpaid tests, custom development disguised as evaluation or POCs without a decision owner.
