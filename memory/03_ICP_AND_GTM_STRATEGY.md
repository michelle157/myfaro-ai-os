# 03 - ICP & GTM Strategy

## Strategic GTM thesis
myFaro should be sold into concrete operating-model pain, not as generic SaaS. The strongest GTM motion is use-case-led: portfolio overview, life and investment operating layer, compliance workflow, pension/80%-rule process, consolidated reporting, advisor-owned client portal or group standardization.

The message must always connect a concrete pain to a concrete operational improvement.

## Primary ICP segments

### Independent insurance brokers with life and investment business
This is the core ICP. Typical profile: 5-15 FTE, meaningful life/investment activity, multiple insurers, Brio/Brokercloud/CRM usage, compliance pressure and reliance on Excel, PDFs and supplier portals for life, pensions and investment advice.

Main pains:
- no full life/investment portfolio overview
- too much manual compliance and reporting work
- insurer portals fragment the process
- pension calculations are inefficient
- reporting is not professional or scalable enough
- compliance depends on individual advisor discipline

Buying triggers:
- FSMA/compliance pressure
- growth in life/investment business
- new leadership or strategic repositioning
- acquisition/consolidation
- desire to professionalize reporting
- frustration with portals and manual documents

Best entry use cases: portfolio overview, compliance workflow, pension/80%-rule, client reporting.

### Bank agents and bank brokers
They have a strong client base but need a broader advisory layer for life, pensions, planning and reporting beyond the banking environment.

Best entry use cases: consolidated reporting, financial planning, pension workflow, advisor-owned client relationship.

### Wealth planners and family offices
They manage high-net-worth or complex clients with multiple asset categories, private markets, companies, real estate and estate structures.

Best entry use cases: consolidated wealth reporting, route plan, private markets visibility, succession and planning workflow.

### Buy-and-build groups
They inherit different CRMs, processes, supplier relations and compliance practices. They need a shared operating model without forcing every office into the same CRM immediately.

Best entry use cases: group operating layer, shared compliance workflow, consolidated reporting, phased rollout by entity.

### Insurers, banks and institutional partners
They want distribution enablement, structured advisor workflows, data/document exchange and more consistent compliance evidence. The partner angle is not “another portal” but a workflow layer that advisors can actually use.

## Secondary ICPs
Accountants, legal/estate planning advisors and asset managers/private market providers can be referral partners or adjacent users, especially around pension, company-owner advice, succession and reporting.

## ICP scoring
Strong fit indicators:
- meaningful life, investment or pension activity
- multi-supplier complexity
- compliance pressure
- AUM or dossier volume to justify license
- internal sponsor with authority
- frustration with manual workflows
- need for reporting or standardization
- strategic need to own the client relationship

Weak fit indicators:
- only wants a cheap CRM or template
- no life/investment activity
- only simple non-life products
- no change appetite
- wants heavy custom development immediately
- no budget or internal owner

## Disqualification reasons
Use: Not in ICP, no life/investment/pension activity, no budget, no urgency, wrong use case, custom-development demand, timing not right, existing vendor lock-in, duplicate/irrelevant.

## GTM motion
The recommended GTM motion combines:

1. referral-led growth
2. LinkedIn thought leadership
3. partner-led credibility
4. event-based authority
5. account-based outreach to high-fit brokers and wealth firms
6. customer expansion and proof-building

This fits a bootstrapped model better than broad paid demand generation.

## Funnel design

### Awareness
Channels: LinkedIn, BZB/Edfin events, webinars, referrals, website, newsletters. Goal: educate the market that independent advisors need an operating layer for life, investments, compliance and reporting.

### Interest
Signals: webinar registration, LinkedIn engagement, website visits via Leadinfo, newsletter subscription, referral intro, event attendance. Goal: convert to discovery.

### Discovery
Goal: identify first high-value use case and ICP fit. Output: pain summary, current systems, business priority, first milestone, demo angle, stakeholder map.

### Demo
Goal: show myFaro around the buyer’s actual use case, not a feature tour. Output: validated use case, objections, decision process and next step.

### Proposal / POC
Translate use case into package, onboarding, discount if applicable, success criteria and implementation path.

### Onboarding and expansion
Reach the first operational milestone quickly, then expand from portfolio to reporting, from reporting to compliance, from pension to broader life workflows, from one office to group rollout.

## Referral strategy
Important referral sources include BZB, Edfin, existing customers, Meester Luc Willems, insurers such as Utmost and OneLife, consultants, accountants and wealth planning experts.

Referral wording:
> We are looking for advisory firms with meaningful life and investment activity that feel the limits of scattered portals, manual compliance and fragmented reporting.

## Partner-led GTM

### BZB / Edfin
Strategic value: broker credibility, compliance authority, webinar/training distribution, BZB discount and link to AML/model documents. Actions: co-branded procedures edition, webinar follow-up, AML day content, attendee outreach.

### Utmost
Strategic value: insurer relationship, data/document exchange, API/DocuSign topic, B-Sure urgency. Guardrail: do not promise final integration unless confirmed.

### OneLife
Strategic value: cross-border life/wealth relevance. Known budget sensitivity around EUR 100k. Use phased partner-enablement framing.

### Deloitte / Covaco / institutional partners
Use operating model transformation, compliance-sensitive distribution and advisor enablement as the anchor, not just product demo.

## Event strategy
Use events for authority and lead activation. Good topics: AML in practice, compliance without unnecessary burden, scalable advisory workflows, CRM not enough, multi-supplier reporting, advisor-owned client relationship.

Post-event workflow: publish recap, send attendee follow-up, offer use-case discovery, segment leads by ICP/urgency, create nurture content from questions.

## Market timing
The timing is strong because compliance pressure is rising, advisory firms consolidate, clients expect better reporting, supplier portals remain fragmented and AI/automation only works when data/workflows are structured. The narrative should be: AI will not fix an unstructured advisory process; the operating layer comes first.
