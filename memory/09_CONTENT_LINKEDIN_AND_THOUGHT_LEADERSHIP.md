# 09 - Content, LinkedIn & Thought Leadership

## Content thesis
myFaro content should create the category in the mind of the market: independent advisors need an operating layer for life, investments, pensions, compliance and reporting. Content should not sound like generic SaaS marketing. It should sound like it comes from someone who deeply understands the advisory business and sees where the market is going.

## Tone of voice
Use a senior, clear, authority-driven, concrete and strategic tone. Long sentences are allowed when they carry insight. For Michelle’s personal profile, the tone may be more vision-driven and founder-led. For the company page, keep the copy shorter, clearer and more use-case oriented.

Avoid generic hooks, exaggerated claims, excessive bullets, too many em dashes, repeated “not X but Y” structures, empty SaaS wording and overuse of words like seamless, unlock, game-changing and innovative.

## Personal profile versus company page

### Michelle personal profile
Use for vision, market observations, contrarian insights, founder perspective, category creation and strategic reflections. It may be more personal and less product-heavy.

### myFaro company page
Use for product use cases, events, customer-facing clarity, partner posts and concise thought leadership. It should remain product-relevant but not pushy.

## Core content pillars

### Operating layer / ERP thesis
Independent advisors do not need another portal; they need a structured operating layer. Angles: why CRM is not enough, why supplier portals fragment advice, why life/investment business needs its own ERP layer, why buy-and-build groups need process standardization.

### Workflow versus documents
Compliance and advice cannot be managed through document lists alone. Angles: documents should be triggered by workflows, irrelevant questions should disappear, compliance should be proportional and embedded, audit trail is created through process rather than paperwork.

### Data aggregation and reporting
Reporting starts with structured data. Angles: no scalable reporting without reliable portfolio overview, multi-supplier advice requires consolidated data, reporting is a relationship moment and the advisor who owns the overview owns the relationship.

### Compliance as scalable process
Compliance should not slow down advice when embedded properly. Angles: AML in practice, AssurMiFID as workflow, audit trail, reproducibility and FSMA-sensitive but non-fear-based communication.

### Pension and 80%-rule
Pension advice is too valuable to manage through scattered portals. Angles: the 80%-rule as recurring friction, corporate pension optimization, insurer portals versus advisor workflow and scalable pension advice.

### Independent advisor control
The advisor should own the client environment. Angles: client login through advisor environment, advisor-branded reporting and the strategic risk of supplier portals owning the relationship.

### Scaling advisory firms
Growth requires repeatable workflows. Angles: staff scarcity, delegation, buy-and-build, process standardization and fewer manual exceptions.

### AI after structure
AI becomes useful only when data and workflows are structured. Angles: AI will not fix an unstructured advisory process; automation creates value only when the workflow is clear.

## Newsletter concepts

### Faro Focus
Audience: prospects, customers and partners. Purpose: thought leadership and advisory operating model. Topics: scaling advisory firms, data aggregation, workflow versus documents, compliance in practice, reporting as client relationship asset.

### Faro Updates
Audience: customers and active users. Purpose: product and release communication. Topics: new features, workflow improvements, training tips, webinars and implementation guidance.

## LinkedIn post structures

### Vision post
Start with a strong market observation, explain why current approach is insufficient, name the operating-model shift, connect lightly to myFaro and end with a clear insight.

### Product insight post
Start from a concrete advisor pain, explain workflow friction, show the workflow principle, mention the myFaro use case and end with an operational outcome.

### Event post
Announce event and topic, explain why it matters now, mention partners/speakers, invite attendance/listening and keep concise.

### Newsletter intro
Use two or three strong framing sentences, introduce the theme, explain why it matters for scaling advisory firms and lead into the article.

## Content workflow
Capture source material from calls, transcripts, events and product updates. Categorize into one of five strategic categories. Extract insight, not only summary. Create LinkedIn post, newsletter or sales asset. Repurpose NL/FR/EN. Store reusable angles. Link content to sales campaign or account list.

## Agent instruction
When creating content: identify the pillar, decide personal versus company voice, keep operating-layer positioning, avoid generic SaaS copy, keep compliance claims careful and make the insight commercially useful.
